# MAITOK Blog Strategy

## Positioning
MAITOK should not present itself as a magical "auto-reply bot." The stronger and more defensible story is: **comment intelligence plus a reply copilot for TikTok sellers.**

That framing is more honest, more useful, and more aligned with current platform/API reality.

## Core audience
1. TikTok Shop sellers drowning in comments
2. Small commerce teams handling DMs/comments manually
3. Agencies and operators who care about speed, tone consistency, and purchase-intent triage
4. Builders interested in the gap between platform promises and real automation constraints

## Narrative pillars

### 1) Comment Intelligence Notes
Posts about what comments really mean in commerce.
- purchase intent
- price questions
- shipping anxiety
- spam vs real buyers
- why sentiment alone is not enough

### 2) Building Tikly in Public
Founder/build-in-public notes.
- product pivots
- GTM hypotheses
- lessons from trying to automate inside platform constraints
- design tradeoffs

### 3) Commerce Reply Systems
Operational / technical posts.
- approval workflows
- reply draft quality
- brand voice controls
- alerting, prioritization, and queue design

## Recommended publishing split
- **Substack (EN):** broader founder / product / market essays
- **Tistory (KR):** practical operator lessons for Korean builders and sellers
- **X:** sharp one-idea posts about platform reality, seller pain, and product positioning

## Content goals for the next 30 days
1. Make the pivot legible: from "automation fantasy" to "useful operator product"
2. Build trust by being honest about TikTok API limitations
3. Attract seller/operator conversations, not just curious builders
4. Establish Tikly as a commerce tool, not a generic AI wrapper

## First 6 post ideas
1. **Why we pivoted from an auto-reply bot to a reply copilot for TikTok sellers**
2. Why sentiment analysis is not enough for commerce comments
3. The 6 comment types that actually matter to TikTok sellers
4. What sellers really want: faster good replies, not fully automatic spam
5. Designing a human-in-the-loop workflow for social commerce replies
6. When browser automation should come after product truth, not before it

## Editorial rules
- Lead with seller pain, not AI novelty
- Be explicit about API/platform constraints
- Prefer workflow insight over abstract "AI future" writing
- Tie every post to a clearer product angle or market insight

## SEO / keyword cluster
- TikTok seller comments
- AI reply copilot
- TikTok comment management
- social commerce customer replies
- TikTok Shop seller workflow
- comment intent analysis

## Suggested cadence
- 1 long EN post per week on Substack
- 1 KR post per week on Tistory
- 2 to 3 X posts per week using comment screenshots / examples / one insight per post

## CTA ladder
- Top of funnel: follow the build-in-public / product notes
- Mid funnel: join a waitlist or request a demo
- Bottom funnel: pilot with real seller comments

## First post recommendation
Start with the pivot story. It immediately explains:
- why MAITOK exists
- what changed in the product thinking
- why platform constraints matter
- why the reply copilot angle is stronger than promising full automation
